XR Production

The Future of
Digital Stages

BY MARCUS VANCE • APRIL 10, 2026 • 10 MIN READ

The "stage" is no longer a physical platform of wood and steel. In 2026, it is a multi-dimensional canvas that blends real-world physics with infinite digital possibilities.

Merging Realities (MR)

Mixed Reality has moved from headsets to the open air. Using ultra-high-density LED volumes and camera tracking systems like Mosys or Stype, we can now create environments that appear to have infinite depth to the human eye. This allows an artist to perform in a "digital cathedral" that responds to their movements in real-time, rendered in Unreal Engine 5.4.

Fig 3.1: XR Stage configuration involving 270-degree LED wrap.

Algorithmic Stagecraft

Stage design is now being assisted by generative AI. By inputting the artist's discography and visual style, our AI systems can generate thousands of unique stage architectures that are physically sound and acoustically optimized. This "Algorithmic Stagecraft" allows for high-concept designs that would have taken human architects months to draft by hand.

"In the future, the only constraint on a stage's design will be the limits of the artist's imagination."

Holographic Teleportation

Using 5G-advanced networks, we are now able to "teleport" guest performers as realistic 3D holograms to stages on the other side of the planet. This isn't just a 2D projection; it's a volumetric stream that allows the digital performer to interact with the physical artist on stage with near-zero latency.

VP of Experimental Tech at PulseEvents. Specialist in XR/AR integration for stadium-tier productions.
