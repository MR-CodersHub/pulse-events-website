Technical Trends

5 Lighting Trends
That Define 2026

BY ALEX RIVERS • FEBRUARY 02, 2026 • 8 MIN READ

The landscape of live event production is evolving faster than ever. In 2026, the barrier between digital and physical space has virtually disappeared, led by innovations in spatial lighting and real-time rendering.

1. Reactive Spatial Arrays

No longer restricted to a fixed rig, 2026 sees the rise of independent, AI-controlled lighting pods that move dynamically based on the artist's position. Using ultra-wideband (UWB) tracking, these lights can follow movement with millisecond precision, creating a personal halo or a focused beam that feels physically attached to the performer.

Fig 1.1: Kinetic lighting array deployed at Pulse Tokyo 2026.

2. Cold-Beam Lasers

Safety has always been a constraint in laser design, but 2026's cold-beam technology allows for high-intensity scans that are completely eye-safe, even in close proximity. This has revolutionized small-venue production, allowing for architectural laser mappings that audience members can literally walk through.

"The goal isn't just to light the stage; it's to sculpt the air around the experience."

3. Holographic Diffusers

By placing holographic film over standard LED fixtures, we can now create "material-free" visuals that appear to float 3-5 feet in front of the source. This creates a depth of field previously only possible with expensive pepper's ghost setups, now available in a touring-friendly rack-mount format.
